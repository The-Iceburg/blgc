What we do

Beach Training

We train regularly throughout the summer months with all our members having access to multiple sessions a week in which we teach a variety of skills. This is all based at our beach based Club HQ @ Joseph Steps, Bournemouth. We also run winter training for our very keen adult members who each Sunday brave the cold water to keep fit.

Learn More

Pool Training

As a club we train in a local swimming pool from September to July every year only stopping over Christmas and Easter. We train on Friday's with sessions starting at 19:30pm and finishing at 22:00pm. We cater for ages 8 years old and up. With dedicated instructors and expert kit, these sessions are a great chance to improve your lifesaving and competition skills

Learn More

IRB Training

Throughout the summer, on a Wednesday night, we train using our amazing inshore rescue boats. These sessions help the more experienced lifeguards at the club improve their IRB skills and knowledge to allow us to provide world-class water saftey cover at local events, such as the BHF pier to pier swim, and compete at an inernational level.

Learn More

About Us

Bournemouth Lifeguard Corps was founded in 1965, moved to Durley Chine in 1967, became a registered charity in 2016 and moved from Durley Chine to Jospeh Steps in 2020. We are affiliated with the Royal Lifesaving Society (RLSS) who are the UK's leading provider of water safety and drowning prevention education and Surf Lifesaving Great Britain (SLSGB) whose aim is to help make our beaches safer and more enjoyable places for everyone. We also work closely with the Royal National Lifeboat Institute (RNLI), working professionally alongside them during major events such as the Bournemouth Air Festival and British Heart Foundation Pier to Pier Swim, for which we provide water safety cover.
